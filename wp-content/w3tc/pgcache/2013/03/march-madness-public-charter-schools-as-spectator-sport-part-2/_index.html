March Madness: Public Charter Schools as Spectator Sport Part 2

In an atmosphere in which the word “public” is often left out of discussions of public charter schools, it might make emotional and/or political sense to champion a bill like LD 533 or 889 that would treat charter schools in a separate and unequal way. But once you accept that these schools are public schools, a reasonable starting place might be the formula used to establish a baseline cost for educating a public school student in Maine. While school funding in general is a complicated process, and adapting such processed for public charter schools can be challenging, Maine’s adaptation of EPS to public charters is seen as among the least aggressive and most equitable of the several methods available.
